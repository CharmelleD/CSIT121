Djèwo Recipe

What is Djèwo?

My name is Charmelle, and on this website, I will share with you my favority dish, Djèwo. Djèwo, also called Amiwo is a traditional, and very popular, dish from my home country, Benin in west Africa. It is a very tasty food, which has the consistency of dough, and which I grew up eating mainly on special occasions. It is a dish generally cooked and served with grilled chicken. My mom taught me how to cook Djèwo as it is normally served, but when I became pescatarian, I came up with my own twist of the recipe.

Ingredients (for 4 people)

  • 4 medium habanero peppers (chopped)
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 4 tbsp of ground smoked shrimp
  • 2 tbsp of onion powder
  • 1 Maggie crevette cube
  • 1.5 cups of vegetable oil
  • 5 cups of crushed tomatoes
  • Some salt and pepper
  • 5 cups of fine courn flour

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Add the crushed tomatoes to a saucepa, and let cook for 10 mins on medium/low heat.
  2. Add garlic powder, ground shrimp, onion powder, Maggie cube (crush it first), salt and pepper, and oil. Mix/stir, and let cook for 2 min.
  3. Add the chopped habanero peppers, and stir.
  4. Add the fine corn flour slowly while stirring the mix.
  5. Let the mix simmer for 15 mins (check and stir every 5 mins).
  6. The Djèwo is ready if it has the consistency of dough.
  7. Serve with pepper sauce (ground cayenne pepper, ground shrimp, vegetable oil, salt and pepper. Dosage is up to you).
  8. Enjoy!

I love this reacipe because it is so easy to cook, and it is delicious. I grew up eating this dish on special occasions like birthdays, christmas, and parties, so subconciously, my brain associated this food with happy memories, and it has become a comfort food for me.
